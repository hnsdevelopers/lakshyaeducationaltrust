Our Journey of 15 Years: A Legacy of Change

Founded in 2009 with just 22 young volunteers, Lakshya has grown into a movement that challenges systemic corruption, empowers marginalized communities, and provides quality education to those who need it most. Our founder, Sanju, started this initiative in the resettlement colonies of East Delhi — Kalyanpuri and Trilokpuri — where basic amenities like clean water, safe public spaces, and functional schools were scarce.

Over the past decade and a half, we have filed hundreds of RTI applications, exposed corruption in government offices, ensured legal action against erring officials, and brought about tangible improvements in public service delivery. Our work has touched thousands of lives, from school children getting midday meals to women finding safer public transport.

Our Core Mission Pillars

These six pillars define our approach towards building a just, educated and empowered society.

Youth Leadership

We believe that youth are not just tomorrow's leaders but today's change-makers. Our leadership development programs train young people in advocacy, public speaking, and civic engagement. Over 3,000 youth have been mentored, many now leading their own community initiatives.

Govt. School Transparency

Through sustained RTI campaigns and community monitoring, we have forced several government schools to open locked toilets, utilize allocated budgets, and improve teaching standards. Our interventions have directly benefited over 5,000 students in East Delhi.

Free Libraries for All

Financial constraints should never be a barrier to knowledge. We have established 8 free community libraries across Delhi-NCR, providing books, newspapers, and competitive exam materials to underprivileged students, benefiting more than 2,500 learners annually.

Environmental Conservation

Our green campaigns include tree plantation drives, waste management awareness, and river conservation projects. We have planted over 10,000 saplings and conducted 50+ awareness workshops in slum clusters about sustainable living.

Rights of Vulnerable Groups

Women, elderly, persons with disabilities and children remain at the center of our rights-based programs. We provide legal aid, health camps, and awareness sessions to ensure they receive government entitlements and live with dignity.

Policy Intervention

We work at the macro level, drafting policy recommendations and collaborating with government bodies to ensure systemic accountability. Our advocacy has led to administrative reforms in public libraries and transport services in Delhi.

Ground Impact Initiatives

Beyond our core pillars, we have executed several high-impact campaigns that have transformed lives and systems. Each initiative stems from grassroots realities and a deep commitment to justice.

RTI for Change – Over 200 RTIs filed exposing corruption in schools, hospitals and municipal offices.
Free Spoken English Classes – 1,200+ underprivileged youth trained for better job opportunities.
EWS & MDM Reforms – Ensured proper implementation of Right to Education and Mid-Day Meal schemes in 15+ schools.
Women's Safety on Blue Line Buses – Forced DTC to deploy 30+ government buses on Route 33, benefitting 50,000+ daily commuters.
Legal battles against corruption – Won landmark cases resulting in suspension of corrupt officials and policy changes.

Library as a Living Hub

Our vision is to establish libraries that go beyond silent reading — they become centers for debate, digital literacy, career guidance and community dialogues. The first such library at Kalyanpuri now serves 500+ active members daily.

Communication & Career Access

We provide free spoken English and soft skills training to youth from economically weaker sections. Many of our alumni have secured jobs in retail, BPO and hospitality sectors, breaking the cycle of poverty.

Fearless Legal Fight

Our legal cell has taken on corrupt officials, private bus mafia, and negligent government departments. We believe in the power of the law to create systemic change. "न झुकना है, न बिकना है, और न रुकना है।"

How We Operate: The Lakshya Model

Lakshya has never taken funding from any government or large corporate donor. Our work is sustained entirely by community trust, small individual donations, and the unwavering commitment of our volunteers. This independence allows us to fearlessly question power and demand accountability without any conflict of interest.

We follow a three-step approach: Identify grievance → Legal/RTI intervention → Community-led follow up. Every campaign begins with listening to the most marginalized voices. We then use legal tools like RTI, public hearings, and court cases to enforce rights. Finally, we train local youth to monitor progress, ensuring long-term sustainability.

"लक्ष्य का सफर" — 15 वर्षों का संघर्ष, समर्पण और सामूहिक प्रयास। यह यात्रा इस बात का प्रमाण है कि दृढ़ संकल्प, सही उद्देश्य और सामूहिक प्रयासों से सबसे कठिन संघर्ष भी सफलता में परिवर्तित किए जा सकते हैं।
